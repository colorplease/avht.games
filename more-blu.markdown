# A Discovery About Tiny Problems
I wanted a creative outlet to try and explain what I was feeling then. It was a really complex feeling, so complex that I had to boil it down to a bunch of little, tiny, insignificant problems. 

I wanted other people to know how I was feeling. It wasn’t as simple as sadness, so I tried to create games that would comfort my past self in how I was feeling.

When I finally began making games, I realized that they were **far** too personal for me to show other people comfortably. Not just that, they were so personal that I felt like it would hinder their experience of the game too. 

Imagine a stranger just cutting themselves open, displaying all of their problems for you to see, when all you signed up for was just to playtest a game.

I needed a way to distance myself and my players from it. I needed a way for players to comfortably bond with the work without the intimacy of my connection to it.


!![Image](/avht.games/images/blu,/kait.jpg)

So I decided to tell a lie.

I told people that this was a game I found while thrifting. I found a laptop that didn’t have the files deleted. There were some games on the laptop. I restored one of them for you to play. That was basically the whole gist of it. Except, right before people would play I would ask them one question.

“I’ve been working on restoring this for months, but I still don’t really understand what it means. Do you think you could help me figure it out?”

!![Image](/avht.games/images/blu,/jonah.jpg)

This got **an amazing** reaction out of people. When people sat down to play the game, they became a lot more emotionally invested within the game. Rather than worrying about the lie itself, they were able to step into the real world fantasy that I created for them.

I was able to get a lot of really raw and emotional statements from people too. About their own experiences and how they related to them in the games. About how they see a little bit of themselves in the unknown author.

It’s a really cool experiment. I can’t wait to release this thing into the wild.
